UX‑SEO Audit Agent

UX‑SEO Audit Agent is an open‑source project that automates the assessment of a website’s user experience (UX) and search engine optimisation (SEO). It combines open‑source tools such as Google Lighthouse with custom HTML parsing and optional AI recommendations to deliver a concise audit. You can use this tool to benchmark your site against a competitor, identify quick wins and prioritise improvements.

Features

  • Combines UX and SEO metrics in a single report. The tool collects titles, meta descriptions, headings, links, images and alt text, measures page speed and weight, and records canonical tags, robots meta, Open Graph metadata, structured data, responsive viewport hints and ARIA attributes for accessibility. It also checks for semantic landmarks like <nav>, <header>, <main> and <footer>, which improve both SEO and screen‑reader navigation.
  • Analyses readability (Flesch score) and tone. It counts words, sentences and syllables to compute a reading ease score, looks for personal pronouns and exclamation marks to infer whether the copy is formal or informal, and even extracts the top keywords on a page.
  • Computes a Layout Score and visual‑hierarchy metrics. Instead of asking you to upload a screenshot, the app derives a score from ratios of headings, images and links relative to the total word count and counts how many semantic landmarks (<nav>, <header>, <main>, <footer>) are present. A balanced ratio (e.g. roughly three headings per thousand words) suggests a clear hierarchy, while extreme values indicate clutter or sparsity. The app displays these ratios alongside the Layout Score and offers suggestions on how to improve your page structure and legibility.
  • Detects conversion and growth signals and computes a Business Score. In addition to counting calls‑to‑action (CTA) buttons or links, scanning for trust‑building words such as “SSL”, “licence” and “avis clients” and tallying the number of forms on the page, the analyser looks for a wider set of business cues: social proof (testimonials, reviews, ratings), promotions and bonuses, explicit benefits, comparison phrases (“vs”, “comparatif”), cross‑sell or recommendation hints, the presence of a FAQ or help section and the availability of a search bar. To make these metrics actionable, it normalises them per 1 000 words and derives a Business Score out of 100 with key performance indicators such as CTA/1 000 words and trust/1 000 words. Ratios outside recommended ranges prompt targeted suggestions. These KPIs and the Business Score appear in the Conversion and Growth tabs, along with the CTA texts, trust keywords and growth signals detected. You can then see at a glance which business elements to add or refine to improve credibility, usability and conversion rate.
  • Highlights content gaps by comparing your page’s top keywords and H2 headings with those of a competitor. If the competitor covers topics or sections you don’t, they appear here as opportunities.
  • Alerts gambling and betting sites to include responsible gaming notices and age restrictions. The tool looks for words like “casino”, “poker” or “jeu” in the domain and, if found, checks whether the page contains a responsible gambling message and an age restriction (18+ or 21+).
  • Notes missing semantic landmarks and low ratios of ARIA labels, encouraging better structure and inclusivity for assistive technologies.
  • Benchmarks against competitor URLs and shows the difference in scores, word counts and readability. You can therefore see at a glance where you outshine your competition and where you fall behind.
  • Zoning & competitor examples. All recommendations are grouped by page zones – Meta, Navigation & Header, Hero & CTA, Main Content, Trust & Social Proof, Growth & Promotions, Footer & Compliance, Accessibility & Semantic, and Design & UX – so you can see which part of the page needs attention. When you provide a competitor URL, the audit highlights where the competitor excels (e.g., more CTAs, stronger trust signals) so you can visualise successful patterns.
  • Presents results in a modern, card‑based interface inspired by Framer. The Streamlit app groups metrics, scores and suggestions into clean panels with consistent typography and soft shadows, making the audit easier to read at a glance.
  • Enhanced result interpretation. Numerical scores are accompanied by qualitative labels (Excellent, Good, Fair, Poor) and short progress bars so you can quickly grasp the overall performance and see how close the page comes to the maximum score. When a competitor URL is provided, a small bar chart compares your page’s UX/SEO, layout and business scores with those of the competitor, highlighting relative strengths and weaknesses at a glance. Detailed raw metrics and lists of call‑to‑action texts or trust keywords are tucked away behind expandable sections, keeping the main view tidy while still providing access to the underlying data.
  • Runs from the command line and integrates with CI pipelines
  • Optional AI‑generated recommendations via OpenAI
  • Exports data as JSON (extendable to other formats)

Quick start

Install dependencies and Lighthouse:

pip install -r requirements.txt
npm install -g lighthouse

Run an audit:

python main.py --url https://example.com --competitor https://competitor.com

To enable AI recommendations set the OPENAI_API_KEY environment variable and pass the --use-openai flag.
